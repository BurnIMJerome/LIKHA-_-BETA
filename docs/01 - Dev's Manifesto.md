# Developer Manifesto

## Why I Built Likha

I've worked with RPA for years.

I've built automations for real businesses, maintained bots running in production, migrated platforms, handled licensing discussions, dealt with broken orchestrators, and spent countless hours explaining why a simple automation suddenly needed another license.

Over time, I realized something:

Automation itself isn't the expensive part.

The ecosystem around it is.

Before many companies even automate their first process, they're already paying for robot licenses, cloud services, AI credits, orchestrators, servers, premium connectors, and training. Then come the yearly renewals, subscription increases, and vendor-specific features that make it difficult to leave.

For many businesses, especially small and medium-sized ones, that's enough to stop their automation journey before it even begins.

That's why I started building Likha.

Not to compete with the biggest RPA platforms.

Not to replace every feature they've built over the last decade.

Just to build something I wished existed when I started.

Something simpler.

Something affordable.

Something developers would actually enjoy using.

## What Is Likha?

Likha is a low-code automation platform for desktop, browser, API, and AI-powered workflows.

It lets you build, run, and monitor automations without forcing you into a specific infrastructure or ecosystem.

You can start by automating a few tasks on your own computer.

When your business grows, Likha grows with you.

You can connect it to a shared database, deploy an on-premise orchestrator, manage multiple bots, or integrate it into your cloud environment.

You decide when it's time to scale.

Not your software vendor.

## Built By Someone Who Uses RPA Every Day

Every feature in Likha exists because I've needed it before.

Every activity has been inspired by real customer projects.

Every workflow pattern comes from problems I've already solved in production.

Instead of adding hundreds of activities that almost nobody uses, I wanted to focus on the ones developers reach for every single day.

My goal isn't to create the biggest automation platform.

It's to create one of the most practical ones.

## Automation Shouldn't Be Locked Behind Expensive Licenses

One thing that has always bothered me about the RPA industry is how difficult it is to get started.

You don't just buy automation software.

You buy everything around it:

- Robot licenses
- Cloud services
- AI subscriptions
- Orchestrators
- Premium features
- Extra modules

Before you've even automated a single process, you've already invested thousands of dollars.

And once you're inside that ecosystem, leaving becomes expensive.

I wanted Likha to be different.

You purchase a license once.

You own your automation platform.

You decide where to run it.

You decide how to scale it.

Most importantly, your automation remains yours.

## Start Small

Not every company needs an enterprise orchestrator.

Sometimes all you need is a single bot running on one computer.

Likha supports that.

Install it.

Build your workflow.

Run it.

That's it.

No cloud setup.

No servers.

No complicated architecture.

If that's all you ever need, that's perfectly fine.

## Scale When You're Ready

Eventually, your automation program might grow.

Maybe you'll need multiple bots.

A centralized queue.

Credential management.

Scheduling.

Logging.

An orchestrator.

Or maybe you want everything running inside your own private infrastructure.

Likha supports that too.

Scale should happen because your business needs it, not because your software requires it.

## AI Without The Middleman

AI is becoming part of almost every automation project.

Unfortunately, many platforms treat AI as another subscription.

Another usage fee.

Another bill.

Likha takes a different approach.

Bring your own AI.

If you already have an API key from OpenAI, Anthropic, Google Gemini, Azure OpenAI, or another provider, connect it directly.

Your requests go straight to your chosen provider.

There are no hidden markups.

No platform commissions.

No extra AI licensing from Likha.

You choose the model.

You control the costs.

You own the configuration.

Likha simply provides the tools to make AI part of your workflows.

## Built To Grow With You

I don't believe software should force you into an architecture you don't need.

Likha works whether you're:

- Automating your own daily tasks
- Building bots for a small business
- Managing automations across multiple departments
- Running enterprise-scale RPA

Start with one machine.

Add a database later.

Deploy an orchestrator when the time is right.

Move to the cloud if your business requires it.

There isn't a "correct" way to use Likha.

There is only the way that makes sense for you.

## My Goal

I'm not trying to build another UiPath.

I'm not trying to build another Power Automate.

Those products solve different problems for different organizations.

What I want to build is an automation platform that removes as much friction as possible.

One that developers enjoy using.

One that businesses can actually afford.

One that doesn't make automation feel like a long-term financial commitment.

If Likha helps someone automate their first repetitive task, save time every day, or build an automation business without spending a fortune on software, then I've achieved exactly what I set out to do.

## The Philosophy

Automation should belong to the people who build it.

You shouldn't rent your own workflows forever.

You shouldn't be forced into an ecosystem just to automate repetitive work.

You shouldn't need enterprise budgets to start automating.

That's what Likha is all about.

Simple.

Flexible.

Affordable.

Built by an RPA developer, for people who just want to automate.

And that's exactly how I intend to keep it.
